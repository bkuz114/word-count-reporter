Reading the Water

	A river never lies, but it rarely speaks plainly. Look for seams—where fast water meets slow. That dark tongue of smooth current slipping past a boulder? That's a trout's dining room. Riffles oxygenate. Pools let a fish think. Learn to see the water not as a single thing, but as a mosaic of micro-habitats.

The best anglers read water like a tired parent reads a bedtime story: slow, patient, and willing to pause on every page.
