The Comment That Prevented a Bug

There is a bug that will never exist because someone wrote a comment.

Not the bug they fixed. The bug that never happened. The edge case no one tripped over. The hour of debugging no one had to do.

You will never know which comment saved which stranger. You will never get a notification. There is no metric for disaster averted.

But somewhere, on a Tuesday night, a tired engineer scrolled past your comment and thought: *oh. right. that.* And they did not introduce the bug you foresaw. And they finished their work. And they went home.

That is the invisible economy of documentation. You pay a small cost now. Someone collects an invisible benefit later. No receipt. No thank you. Just a world with slightly less frustration in it.
