# Documentation as Quiet Legacy

You will leave things behind. Code that outlives your tenure. Functions that run after you have moved on. Systems you built that someone else will maintain.

What will they think of you?

Not whether you were clever. Not whether you used the right design pattern. Whether you *cared*. Whether you thought about the person after you.

Good documentation says: *I was here. I knew you might struggle. I tried to help.*

That is not engineering. That is kindness. And it lasts longer than any architecture decision.

Write for the person who inherits your work. Write like you are leaving a note in a bottle. Someone will find it. Someone will understand. Someone will be grateful, even if they never know your name.
